What speical on this day at Raskot?

The Paith festival in Raskot, Nepal is a cultural and religious celebration that dates back generations. It is headed by local priests, and they bust into a traditional rhythmic telegraph dance where each track seems to be rehearsed. It is a special dance which makes this day unique, symbolizing the religious bond of the community with their gods. Some of the villagers offer garlands, incense sticks and raw rice as a mark of reverence to Goddess Polemma for divine blessings with family wellbeing.

Priests say prayers in the name of not only themselves at but also for families who may be unable to attend on festival day, so everyone is included spiritually with resulting benefits. The offerings are made collectible to the ancestors and gods rather than as a measure of any intent. A sacred environment abounds in the religious air with prayers, which echo through chants and rituals that promotes their culture and spiritual belief.

Apart from the religious aspect, Paith is a another social injection togetherness and stalls for serving native cuisines by local vendors. The energy of the place pulls large gatherings that makes a assembling marketplace around these spiritual events. The distinct amalgam of faith, culture and community spirit are elements by which the local society distinguishes itself from other communities nearby and hence have succeeded in turning Paith into a focal event for Raskoties such that now it is not only celebrated as occasion to honor gods but also eyes upon celebrations.
